## Best Practices — Study Area

1. **Use the finest spatial resolution your data supports.** If you have gridded concentration data at 0.01° and admin-2 boundaries, use admin-2 rather than aggregating to admin-1. Finer resolution captures within-country variation.

2. **Align boundaries with reporting units.** Choose administrative units for which population, mortality, and health data are routinely reported. This simplifies data collection and makes results actionable for policymakers.

3. **Document your spatial choices.** Report the boundary source, resolution, and any simplifications made. This supports reproducibility and transparency (Anenberg et al., 2016).

4. **Start with PM2.5 if unsure.** It has the strongest evidence base, the most CRF options, and the largest attributable health burden globally. You can add pollutants in subsequent analyses.

5. **Use multi-year averages for chronic CRFs.** Chronic-exposure CRFs (most PM2.5 mortality CRFs) assume long-term mean exposure. A 3–5 year average concentration is more appropriate than a single year.

6. **Match year to the most recent overlapping data.** Identify the most recent year for which concentration, population, and incidence data all exist. This minimizes temporal mismatch.
